Empathy · Research · Insight

Why we created HeritageVerse.

During our DTI empathy work, we spoke with students, teachers and heritage lovers. Many felt that history is “far away” — trips are rare, guides are rushed, and textbooks don’t convey feeling. We wanted to change that.

Refined Problem Statement

How might we make historical learning immersive, accessible and multilingual so that learners can emotionally connect with heritage sites, even when they cannot travel there physically?

Temple Courtyard · Calm Space

We imagine quiet cultural spaces that can be entered through AR/VR, guided with respectful narration.

Key insights from our empathy research

Some of the repeated pain points we heard during interviews and observation:

  • School trips are limited by time, money and distance.
  • Guides, when available, cannot slow down for every student.
  • Language barriers reduce understanding for many visitors.
  • Textbook lessons feel flat and not memorable.

These insights led us to an opportunity: use AR/VR to recreate spaces, and an AI guide to adapt explanations to age, language and curiosity. Instead of replacing real visits, HeritageVerse acts as a bridge and a rehearsal.
