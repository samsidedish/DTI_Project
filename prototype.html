Prototyping · Testing

From sketches to a walkable experience.

As part of DTI, we started with a rough paper prototype to organise screens and flow. Then we moved to a higher-fidelity mockup to simulate how a learner selects a site, mode and language before entering the tour.

User testing snapshot

Students who interacted with the prototype used words like “movie-like”, “fun” and “easier to imagine”. We used their feedback to refine sequence, labels and narration prompts.

Courtyard & Arches · Test Scene

Our prototype focused on a simple but atmospheric scene to evaluate comfort and clarity of navigation.

Prototype flow

The current journey in our prototype is:

  • Open the HeritageVerse app and browse heritage sites.
  • Select a monument and choose AR (phone) or VR (headset) mode.
  • Pick a preferred language and meet the AI guide.
  • Walk through the scene while the guide points out key elements.
  • Finish with a short recap and quick quiz for recall.

We evaluated the prototype on ease of use, level of immersion, clarity of narration and whether learners felt more connected to the place compared to a textbook page. The next step is to convert this into a working digital prototype with basic AR/VR integration.
